Ingredients

1 1/2 cups neutral oil ( I use corn)

2 large shallots, thinly sliced

8 cloves of garlic, thinly sliced

1/2 cup Chinese red chili flakes

2 Tbsp gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes)

3/4 tsp salt

1 Tbsp sugar

1 tsp msg (optional)

1 Tbsp soy sauce

1 Tbsp sesame seeds, toasted

Pour oil into a medium saucepan, add the shallot and garlic.  Turn the heat to medium high and continue to stir until shallot and garlic are slightly browned.  You will see the oil bubbling, but this can take up to 15- 20 minutes depending on the oil temperature. 

While this is cooking, mix both chili flakes, sugar, salt, and msg in a heat proof container.  I use stainless steel as the high temperature can cause glass or porcelain to crack. 

Remove crispy shallots and garlic from oil, set aside. Bring the oil temperature up to 350 degrees.  Pour hot oil over chili flake mixture. Stir to combine, then add the soy sauce, toasted sesame seeds, the crispy garlic and shallots.  Mix and enjoy.  Store this covered in the refrigerator.
