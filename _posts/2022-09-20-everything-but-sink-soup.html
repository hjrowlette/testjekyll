 This is a riff off a great family classic--lentil soup! It has a lot of the same elements, but I've just gotten in the habit of throwing in whatever vegetable scraps, meat leftovers, or spices I'm in the mood for! Plus we don't have the original lentil soup on the blog so it's way past time it was added. It's super easy and quick and so easy to make your own. enjoy :)


Everything but the Sink Soup

Ingredients

1 lb lentils (green, french, red), rinsed and drained

8 cups water or stock (veg, chicken, etc)

1 large chopped onion

EVOO

4-6 minced garlic

1 lb smoked sausage, coined

cooked/wilted spinach or kale

sliced carrots

can of roasted diced tomatoes

1 tsp oregano, italian seasoning, or other herbs

salt and pepper

miso

balsamic vinegar

gochujang


Directions

1. In a dutch oven or large pot, brown the onion in olive oil over medium heat. Once softened, stir in minced garlic, cooking for another minute or two.  Season with salt and pepper

2. Add spinach, carrot, and sausage; cook for another few minutes.

3. Add canned tomatoes, rinsed lentils, water or stock, herbs, and other seasonings as desired. Season again with salt and pepper.

4. Bring to a boil; reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes. Taste and make any final adjustments--enjoy!
