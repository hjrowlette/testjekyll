Tried and True recipe full of flavor and spice this has quickly become a family fave.  Marinate this chicken overnight and you will be congratulating yourself the next day! The original recipe calls for a whole chicken, but you can cut your chicken in half for faster cooking, or just "pick a part" because any piece of chicken will be delish with this marinade and sauce to back it up. Recommend bone in, skin on chicken.  Chicken can be oven baked or grilled. We have done both. Larger breasts, half chickens, and whole chickens can be cooked with indirect heat on the grill for less work and more social time.  Also, try the whole chicken roasted upright over a half of a beer (spray beer can with Pam). Serving this with rice or naan bread to soak up any remaining sauce on your plate is always a winner with this chicken dinner!



Peruvian Chicken ingredients for marinade:  (from Cooking Illustrated Peruvian                                                                                                      Chicken with Garlic and Lime)


1/4 cup fresh mint leaves
3 Tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
1 Tbsp salt
1 Tbsp pepper
1 Tbsp cumin
1 Tbsp sugar
2 tsp smoked paprika
2 tsp dried oregano
2 tsp finely ground lime zest 
1/4 cup lime juice
1 tsp minced habanero (use gloves!)

4 lbs of chicken

Directions:

Process all ingredients, except the chicken, in the blender until a smooth paste forms, 10-20 seconds. Use finger to gently loosen skin covering breast and thighs; place half of the paste under the skin, directly on the meat.  Spread remaining paste over the skin.  Place chicken in a ziploc bag or bowl covered with plastic for 6 hours or up to 24 hours. 

If baking, bake the chicken at 325 until skin is golden and breast registers 140 degrees. Remove chicken from oven and increase the temperature to 500 degrees. Continue roasting until chicken is completely cooked (breast 160, thigh 175) and skin is crispy brown. Cover with foil if browning too quickly.  If grilling, cook until chicken is done using indirect heat for whole, half, or large breasts and direct heat for legs, thighs, and smaller breasts.  Or you can use a combination of any of these cooking methods.  After your chicken is cooked, let it rest before serving.  While the chicken is cooking, move onto the sauce!

The cooking method will not make or break this dish so use works best for you, your family, friends, and the occasion.  Try it different ways and decide what you like best!

1/2 cup extra-virgin olive oil (as desired, we only added 1/4 cup)
 Lime wedges, for serving garnish

Directions:

In a blender, combine all of the above ingredients EXCEPT olive oil. Process until smooth.  With blender running slowly add the olive oil until sauce becomes emulsified.  We added only 1/4 cup of oil and were happy with the taste and texture. Try 1/4 cup, then continue adding the additional 1/4 cup until you are a satisfied with the taste.

Carve that chicken, slather it up with that spicy sauce, and, before you drool...  take a bite!!
