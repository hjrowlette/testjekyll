I believe that this post is the first of it's kind, it's dedicated entirely to vegetables! I love veggies, I can't get enough. What most people consider enough veggies to feed a family I consider enough for me and Michael. It's a problem, but it's the right kind of problem to have! I read somewhere that if you don't like a vegetable, it's not being cooked right. So to all those brussel sprout and kale haters out there, ya crazy! That being said, even I have my limits (looking at you okra and lima beans). There are a couple of small recipes and theories that I want to highlight. The first is kale.



Kale. Can. Be. Good. I promise! I have taken to buying 1lb bags of it and it makes a great dinner, smoothie, or salad addition. I buy it pre prepped, so all of the large bitter stems have already been removed. It's washed and ready to rock and roll! I've been cooking it in my stainless steel pan. I'll get it really hot and add a healthy dollop of olive oil. Once the oil shimmers add as much sliced garlic as you (and the pan) can handle. Just before the garlic starts to brown I'll add several heavy handfuls of kale and let it set. This lets it get brown on the bottom, depending on how wet the kale is sometimes this goes better than others... Once it starts to wilt I'll generously season with salt and pepper give it a good turn and as it cooks down if it's not enough I'll add another handful. Once it's all wilted finish with some fresh lemon and crushed red pepper. Yum!
 

Okay, veggie round #2. Broccoli. Our ever beloved BA gets credit for this idea. The concept is simple, crunchy brown broccoli paired with lemon, red pepper, and...wait for it...cheddar cheese. I made this for the first time this weekend and my life is forever changed! Cut broccoli into bite sized pieces and get either a cast iron or stainless steel pan real hot. Add a few table spoons of olive oil and once it shimmers arrange the broccoli into a single layer, cut side down. Season with salt and pepper. Now here is where I went off the reservation per the BA brain child. I had a very simple problem. In order to make sure that my broccoli got crispy I didn't shove all that I had prepped into the pan. Because as everyone knows, an overcrowded pan don't crisp, it steams! SO. Once my first batch was brown but still very crisp, I pulled it off and set it on a cookie sheet. I browned my second batch and preheated my oven to 350. Once all the broccoli was a little charred and still very crunchy. I crumbled sharp white cheddar all over and followed behind it with a few heavy shakes of crushed red pepper. I let it bake for just about 10 min and finished it under the broiler. Once it came out I finished with lemon juice. O.M.G. Revolutionary! It's like a healthier, more adult take on broccoli with cheese sauce.
 

Round 3! Clearly there is a trend in this post: browned veggies. And this recipe stays true to that concept! By now we all know and love our oven roasted broccoli. This takes that same concept, but with brussel sprouts! Wash and prep the sprouts by cutting them in half. Place in a single layer on a cookie sheet and toss with EVOO, salt and pepper. Roast at 400 until delightfully brown, about 20 min. While the sprouts are wrapping up in a small Tupperware container mix equal parts lemon juice, maple syrup (the real stuff!), and olive oil, salt and pepper. Typically I do a tablespoon of each. Once the sprouts are done, shake the container until the mixture is emulsified. Drizzle on the roasted sprouts and enjoy! I've been using this concept for quite a while and this vinaigrette is really good on any cruciferous vegetable. I love it on broccoli!

Cruciferous Vegetables

 

The last one. This is a Hannah brain child, but I think it belongs in this post as it has found a home in my heavy vegetable rotation. Green beans with shallot and lemon. It's very simple, wash and cut the beans into bite sized pieces. Roast in a pan on the stove (or heck, try the oven!), with EVOO, salt and pepper. Once they just start to get soft add in 2 sliced shallots and cook until they are opaque. I like to try to get my beans really browned before I add in the shallots. Pull off the heat and finish with a heavy squeeze of lemon.

 

There are several trends in all these posts. Take a veggie, any veggie. Add high heat, plenty of olive oil and seasoning. Cook until brown, finish with any combo of lemon juice, cheese, crushed red pepper, or try them all! I haven't found a veggie that it doesn't work with yet (except you okra, you will never taste good).

Until next time, happy eating!
