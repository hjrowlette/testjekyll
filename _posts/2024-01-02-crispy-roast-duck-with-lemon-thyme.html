This was such a fun NYE experiment, and Hunter was a great sport to join in! A few notes--our bird was only 4.6lb and took 30 minutes less than was outlined below. I would recommend after about 2 hours you start temperature checking your bird, it needs to at least reach 165F before you glaze it and bake the final 15 minutes.

I will also say when you first put the duck in the hot oven be prepared for a lot of spitting and smoking, and have your kitchen hood/fans going. The duck fat begins rendering really quickly and goes a little nuts in a 475 oven. When we applied the glaze we did the final 15 minutes with the glaze at 425, and we switched to a fresh pan to reduce the risk of more smoking/burning the pan drippings.

I would consider doing a dijon mustard vinaigrette to accompany the potatoes, but any extra glaze is also delicious on the potatoes, shallots, and pears!

Finally, I didn't include figs in the recipe. They aren't in season right now and the store just didn't have any. I'm sure it would be fine with or without them, so if you give it a go with the figs included let me know how it goes!


Crispy Roast Duck

 

Ingredients

1 (5 to 6 pound) whole duck

½ pound baby potatoes

3 shallots

6 fresh figs – optional-

5 Bartlett pears

2 tablespoons butter

4 lemons, divided

5 tablespoons honey, divided

Kosher salt

Freshly ground pepper

1 bunch fresh thyme

 

Directions

1. Preheat oven to 475 F.

2. Take the duck out of the refrigerator 30 minutes before preparing it.

3. Cut the baby potatoes, shallots, three figs, and three pears in half. Season the potatoes and shallot with salt and pepper. Arrange everything on an oiled baking sheet. Put the tray in the oven and roast 30 minutes while you prepare the duck and fruit stuffing.

4. Melt the butter and add it to a mixing bowl. Mix in the juice of 1 ½ lemons and 1 tablespoon of honey. Cut the remaining figs and pears and half a lemon into eighths, and add them to the lemon-butter mixture.

5. Using a sharp knife, score the duck's skin in a crosshatch pattern. Avoid cutting into the duck's meat. Generously season the duck inside and out with kosher salt and freshly ground pepper. Trim any excess fat around the duck's cavity. Stuff the duck's cavity with the fruit stuffing, then pour in the lemon juice from the bowl. Criss-cross the duck's legs, and secure with kitchen twine. Place the duck breast-side up on a rack inside of a roasting pan.

6. Put the duck into the preheated oven, and roast for 10 minutes. Lower the temperature to 300 degrees, and roast the duck for 50 minutes.

7. Turn the duck breast-side down, and roast for 30 minutes. Turn the duck breast-side up, and roast 30 more minutes. Temperature check your duck; the safe cooking temperature is 156F. If needed (likely necessary for larger bird) once more turn the duck breast-side down, and roast 30 more minutes. 

8. While the duck is roasting, combine the juice of 2 lemons, 4 tablespoons of honey, and the fresh thyme in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over medium heat until the thyme turns brown. Remove the thyme, lower the heat, and simmer until thickened, about 10-15 minutes.

9. Take the duck out of the oven, and brush the breasts with the lemon-honey-thyme glaze. Save excess glaze to serve with the duck and sides.

10. Increase the oven temperature to 475 degrees. If there is a lot of drippings in your pan, clean out or transfer duck and rack to a clean dish.  Once preheated, roast the duck a final 10-15 minutes. Watch the duck carefully so the glaze doesn't burn.

11. Take the duck out of the oven and transfer it to a cutting board. Let it rest 15 minutes before carving. While the duck is resting, put the potatoes, shallots, figs, and pears back in the oven to reheat. 

12. Cut up or carve the duck, and serve with the potatoes, shallots, figs, and pears. I didn't eat the fruit stuffing, but in theory you can.
