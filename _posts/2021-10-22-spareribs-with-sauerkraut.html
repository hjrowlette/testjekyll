--- layout: post title: Spareribs with Sauerkraut date: '2021-10-22T18:39:00.000-04:00' author: Nathalie tags: modified_time: '2021-10-22T18:39:33.920-04:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-2383701181948463629.post-3073102212426868910 blogger_orig_url: https://rowlettechefs.blogspot.com/2021/10/spareribs-with-sauerkraut.html --- A family favorite for years, this recipe comes from the cookbook Best of America by Carla Calpalbo and Laura Washburn published in 1994.  I purchased this cookbook in the basement of the Naval Annex in Washington DC while working at Headquarters Marine Corps.  It has several mainstay recipes that have frequented the Rowlette table for years including this one from Midwestern America, which fits right in with Bobs' German heritage.

Ingredients

3-4     lbs pork spareribs, cut in individual portions ( I like the country style for this recipe)
1/4     cup minced onion (1 small)
1/4     cup Worcestershire sauce
1        Tbsp dry mustard
1/2     tsp paprika
1        tsp salt
3        cups flat beer (or perhaps cider)
3        Tbsp olive or vegetable oil
1.5    quarts of sauerkraut, drained
1        tart/sweet apple, peeled, cored, and sliced
1        tsp caraway seeds

Directions 

Arrange ribs in a single layer in large baking dish

In a large measure, combine onion, worcestershire, mustard, paprika, salt, and beer.  Mix well. Pour over ribs. Marinate a minimum of two hours though overnight is best. Baste or turn ribs occasionally.

Preheat oven to 375

Remove ribs from dish and pat dry, reserving marinade.

Heat oil in flameproof casserole or dutch oven.  Working in batches, brown ribs on all sides. Pour in the marinade.  Transfer casserole to the oven and bake for 35-40 minutes. Turn ribs occasionally.

Rinse sauerkraut, if desired, and drain well. Mix with apple and caraway seeds. 

Remove casserole from oven, pushing ribs to one side distribute sauerkraut on the bottom of the dish. Arrange the ribs on the top and push down evenly in the kraut.

Return to the oven and bake until the meat on the ribs is fall off the bone tender 3/4 - 1 hour more.
