This is a GREAT one! super fast, super tasty, but definitely a LIGHT meal.  A single recipe is either a big meal for one person or a small meal for two, but any more than two people and I would just triple the recipe. It's a variation on socca pancakes which Britt certainly knows and loves, and it's gonna be a staple in my kitchen since I now have 4lbs of chickpea flour!

Ingredients

1/2 cup chickpea flour

3 Tbsp. plus 4 tsp. olive oil, divided

Kosher salt

1/2 medium red onion, thinly sliced

4 garlic cloves, smashed

1 cup brussels sprouts, thinly sliced

1 small bunch Tuscan kale or other kale, ribs and stems removed, thinly sliced

2 tsp. hot sauce, plus more for serving

4 oz. sharp cheddar, coarsely grated

Plain yogurt (for serving)

 

Directions

1. Preheat oven to 350°.

2. Whisk chickpea flour and ½ cup plus 2 Tbsp. cold water in a small bowl. Whisk in 1 Tbsp. oil and a pinch of salt. Let sit at least 10 minutes and up to 1 hour to allow flour to hydrate.

3. Meanwhile, heat 2 Tbsp. oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high. Add onion, garlic, and brussels sprouts; cook, tossing occasionally, until softened and lightly browned, about 3 minutes. Add kale and cook, tossing often, until tender, about 3 minutes. Add 2 tsp. hot sauce, season with salt, and toss well. Transfer to a medium bowl; wipe out skillet.

4. Swirl 1 tsp. oil in skillet to coat; heat over medium-high. Pour ¼ cup batter into center of skillet and swirl to form a thin 6"–7"-diameter pancake. Cook until well browned underneath and crispy around edges, about 2 minutes. Flip pancake and cook just until second side is lightly browned, about 1 minute. Transfer to a rimmed baking sheet. Repeat process with remaining batter and 3 tsp. oil to make 4 total.

5. Divide vegetables between 2 pancakes and top with cheese and remaining 2 pancakes. Bake until cheese is melted, 6–8 minutes. Serve topped with yogurt and more hot sauce.
