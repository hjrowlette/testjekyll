This recipe has become a family icon--I think Mom and Dad were the first to make it, and it is too tasty for words.  The prep fast, the cook time is faster, and the flavor packs a punch.  Be careful not to oversalt and you can't go wrong!


Alton Brown Fish Tacos
Ingredients
for the fish
-3 cloves garlic
-1 cup packed cilantro leaves
-2 limes, zested (save the limes for serving)
-2 tsp ground cumin
-1 1/2 tsp kosher salt (go light here, its easy to oversalt this recipe)
-1 tsp black pepper
-1/4 cup tequila
-1 lb tilapia fillets
-1 tbsp olive oil

to serve
-tortillas
-shredded red cabbage
-lime wedges
-crema (recipe below)

for crema
-chipotle pepper in adobo sauce
-heavy cream

Directions
1. Put garlic, cilantro, lime zest, cumin, salt, and pepper in a small food processor and pulse until combined.  With processor running, add tequila.  Place tilapia fillets in 1-gallon resealable bag, add the garlic mixture and move around to coat each fillet.  Let marinade at room temperature for 15-20 minutes

2. Heat cast-iron pan over high heat, brushing with olive oil. Cook fillets until just cooked through and opaque, about 3-4 minutes per side.  Do not overcrowd the pan, if necessary cook in 2 batches.

3.  Cut fish into strips and serve with warm tortillas with crema, shredded red cabbage, and lime wedges.

directions for crema (different from alton brown's recipe)
1. Using a Vitamix/ Nutrininja small blender (or the food processor attachment on an immersion blender), combine 1 cup of heavy cream with one or two chipotle chiles plus some adobo sauce. Blend until a whipped cream forms; taste and add more chipotle or sauce until your desired level of spiciness is achieved.
