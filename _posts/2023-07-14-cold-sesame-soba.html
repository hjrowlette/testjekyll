 It's summer! It's hot! You don't wanna turn the oven on--heck, you don't even wanna be on the kitchen! Enter: cold sesame soba. Delicious, meant to be eaten cold, and entirely too easy to throw together in only the time it takes to boil a pot of water. This is a great lunch dish, or could be paired with an Asian style salad, some sushi, or even a cheeky bowl of edamame to make it a bigger meal. *cough cough I did all those things and it was amazing*

The sauce for these is ridiculously easy, and the only thing to chop is cucumbers and scallions. that's it! enjoy!

Also I have to plug it, this recipe is courtesy of Don't Panic Pantry, a cookbook I'm obsessed with from this awesome chef who also does a lot of youtube videos, so you don't just have to take my word for it, you can watch the recipe author, Noah, make the dish here! https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YDIBx90auKA

Cold Sesame Soba

Serves 2 as a main dish, 3-5 as a side


 Ingredients

1/4 cup tahini

3 tbsp EVOO

1 tbsp Chile Oil or Sesame Oil

1 tbsp plus 1 tsp tamari (could substitute soy sauce)

1 tbsp plus 1 tsp rice vinegar

1 tbsp plus 1 tsp red wine vinegar

12.7 oz soba noodles

1 english cucumber or 4 persian cucumbers, cut into matchsticks (small and thin)

2 scallions, sliced

1 tbsp white sesame seeds

 

Directions

1.  Bring a large pot of water to boil for the soba

2. In a large mixing or serving bowl combine the tahini, EVOO, chile oil, tamari, red wine vinegar, and rice vinegar, and stir until combined. It may look a little broken but it’ll come together

3. One water is boiling, cook soba according to package directions. Drain soba and rinse under cold water until no longer hot (room temp – cold is the goal). Shake the sieve to remove as much water as possible from the noodles to avoid diluting the sauce

4. Add drained noodles to large bowl with the dressing and toss until noodles are coated. Season with salt as needed. Mix in cucumbers, scallions, and sesame seeds (could also garnish individual bowls). Eat immediately, leftovers are good for about 5 days.
