 This recipe from a Campbells Soup Cookbook  (yes, you read that correctly!) was a mainstay while these kids were growing up.  Easily made ahead, I think most of them liked it.  This is not something I make much these days but I think a couple of you creep back to this occasionally so here it is, just in case! This recipe gave options so I included them all but we always did cheddar, ham, and peas!


Ingredients

1 can condensed soup  (cheddar cheese, tomato,  cream of mushroom, cream of onion)

1/3 cup of milk

1 Tbsp parsley

2 cups cooked rice

Protein ( 2 cups diced cooked ham, 2 cups diced cooked beef, 1 can kidney beans or 1 can drained                         salmon)

1 cup Vegetable ( peas, drained cooked corn, drained cooked spinach, chopped tomato)

1 cup of cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, longhorn, or parmesan (just 1/4 cup) 


Directions

In a large bowl, combine soup, milk and parsley, stir to blend. Stir in the rice, protein, and vegetable.  Turn mixture into a baking dish, sprinkle with cheese.

Bake at 350 for 30 minutes or until heated through.  Makes about 7 cups
