 This is an updated recipe from Claire Saffitz's dessert cookbook--I wish I liked her cookbook more, but there were a lot of recipes I wasn't interested in, and I felt like I had to make significant alterations to other recipes. This one originally was tragically overbaked, so I reduced the cooking time as well as increased the orange flavors to try and make that more of a noticeable element. I wish the cake used a little less vegetable oil as it's a little boring, but I won't deny it lends itself to a very moist and tender cake as a result. Overall the result is an easy to make, super moist, fragrant and classic bundt that I very much enjoy!



Almond Poppy Seed Bundt Cake

 

Ingredients

Neutral oil and all-purpose flour for pan

2 1/3 cups granulated sugar (465g)

2 tbsp poppy seeds (17g)

1 1/2 tsp baking powder (6g)

1 tsp kosher salt (3g)

3 cups all-purpose flour (390g)

1 1/2 cups whole milk (360g)

1 1/3 cups neutral oil (veg or grapeseed) (288g)

3 large eggs

1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

1 1/2 tsp almond extract

Zest of 1 orange

 

Glaze

3/4 cup powdered sugar (90g)

1/4 cup orange juice (57g)

2 tsp butter, melted

1/2 tsp vanilla extract

1/2 tsp almond extract

1/2 tbsp Cointreau (optional)

 


Directions

1. Preheat oven to 350F. Brush inside of bundt pan with oil, dust flour around bottom, sides, and up the tube. Tap out excess flour and set aside.

2. In a small bowl combine the 1/3 cup of the granulated sugar with orange zest. Rub with fingers to release orange oil until sugar is fragrant and lightly colored orange.

3. In a large bowl combine granulated sugar (using the 1/3 cup of orange sugar and rest plain), poppy seeds, baking powder, salt, and flour.

4. Add milk, oil, eggs, and vanilla and almond extracts to the bowl and beat on low just to combine. Increase speed to medium-high and beat until batter is very smooth and thick, about 2 minutes. If using a hand mixer give it an additional minute or so.

5. Scrape batter into the bundt pan and bake until top is risen, split, and deep golden brown. Cakeâ€™s internal temp should be about 210F, about 60-70 minutes. Let cake cool in the pan for 15 minutes

6. Use a butter knife or offset spatula to release edges of bundt pan. Invert onto wire rack and tap to release cake. Using a skewer or toothpick poke holes over the top of the cake. Set wire rack over rimmed baking sheet to catch glaze drips and set aside.

7. In a medium bowl, whisk all glaze ingredients until smooth. Brush all of the glaze over the top of the cake, allowing it to absorb. Let cool completely.
