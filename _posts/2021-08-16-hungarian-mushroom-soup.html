Prepare yourself for the ultimate mushroom soup! This stuff is so good, it's got tons of alliums, tons of herbs, and tons of, you guessed it, mushrooms! It comes together relatively quickly, the longest part is prepping all the veggies!

Ingredients

4 medium yellow or white onions (about 1½ lb.)

2 lb. mushrooms (crimini and/or shiitake)

2 medium carrots (about 12 oz.)

6 garlic cloves

2 handfuls of parsley leaves with tender stems

1 stick unsalted butter

4 tsp. Hungarian sweet paprika, plus more for serving

4 tsp. dried dill or ¼ cup fresh dill

2 tsp. dried thyme or 2 Tbsp. fresh thyme

6 cups low-sodium vegetable broth

2 cups whole milk

1/4 cup all-purpose flour (or GF C4C)

3/4 cup sour cream, plus more for serving

Freshly ground black pepper

Kosher salt


Directions

1.  Prepare ingredients; chop onions in 1/4" pieces. Clean mushrooms, remove and discard stems (I recommend saving for another recipe or stock!) and slice 1/4" thick. Peel carrots, cut into 4 long spears, and cut crosswise into 1/4" thick pieces. Mostly just try and get everything to an even thickness! Mince garlic, chop parsley, and chop dill or thyme if using fresh.

2. Melt butter in pot or dutch oven over medium heat. Add onion and a pinch of kosher salt, and cook until onion is softened and browned, about 10 minutes. Add mushrooms and carrots to pot and cook until mushrooms start to brown 12-16 minutes.

3. Add garlic, paprika, dill, thyme, and half of parsley. Cooking until fragrant, about 2 minutes.

4. Increase heat to high and add 6 cups of vegetable broth; bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 10 minutes

5.  Whisk milk and flour together in a small bowl; pour into soup and stir well. Increase heat to high and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for about 8 minutes, until soup can thinly coat a spoon. Remove from heat

6. Mix sour cream and 2-3 tbsp broth from soup in a small bowl until sour cream has loosened, then add back into soup and stir to incorporate. Season with salt and freshly ground pepper.

7. Ladle into bowls and top with a dollop of sour cream, some of the reserved parsley, and a pinch of paprika.
