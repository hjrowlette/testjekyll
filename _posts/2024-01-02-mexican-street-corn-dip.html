I've seen different versions of this recipe online for a while and I've been wanting to try one forever! Finally decided to test one out and with a few tweaks its definitely a winner. It's super easy to make a smaller portion and halve the recipe, just make sure to do 2 cans of corn. It's easy to adjust the spice to whatever you want, just add more jalapeno/serrano. It's a great opportunity to use a frozen hot pepper and a microplane to keep things super easy. Enjoy!

Mexican Street Corn Dip

(recipe is easily halved, but use 2 cans of corn)

Ingredients

16 ounces 2 blocks low fat cream cheese, softened

1/2 cup sour cream

2 cloves garlic minced

2 tablespoons franks redhot sauce, cholula, or other hot sauce

2 tablespoons juice from one lime

2 cups shredded pepper jack cheese divided

3 cans of corn, fully drained and rinsed

4 ounces low fat feta cheese (or Cotija Cheese, crumbled)

1 jalapeno or serrano pepper chopped or microplaned (leave the seeds in for extra spice, or remove the seeds for a milder flavor)

2 tablespoons red onion finely chopped

1/2 cup fresh cilantro chopped

Your favorite chips for dipping

 

Directions

1. Preheat oven to 350F

2. In a blender or food processor (or using a mixer), combine softened cream cheese, sour cream, garlic, hot sauce, lime juice, and 1 cup of the shredded cheese. Blend until fully combined.

3. Add the remaining one cup cheese, the corn, feta, pepper, onion, and cilantro. Stir to combine.

4. Pour mixture into a prepared baking dish. Sprinkle with more cheese if desired.

5. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until cheese is hot and bubbly. If desired, garnish with more cilantro, feta, and hot sauce.

6. Serve with chips and enjoy!
