Chicken-Lentil Soup with Jammy Onions

4 boneless chicken thighs, patted dry
Salt
1/4 c. EVOO
1 1/2 large onions, thinly sliced
1/2 onion diced
6 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
1 cup lentils, rinsed
1 tsp. ground turmeric
1/2 tsp. crushed red pepper
Ground black pepper
Juice of one lemon
Plain greek yogurt (full fat, obviously) for serving


Directions:

Heat oil over medium-high meat in medium pot. Add sliced onion, season with salt. Cook stirring occasionally, until golden brown, 12-15 minutes. Add garlic and cook, stirring often, until garlic is golden and onion is deep golden brown and lightly charred. Remove pot from heat and transfer onion and garlic to a plate with a slotted spoon, set aside for serving. Return pot to medium-high heat, add lentils and turmeric. Cook, stirring a few times, until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Add chicken, diced onion, red pepper flakes and 6 cups of water. Season generously with salt and pepper. Bring to simmer and then reduce to medium-low. Cook stirring occasionally, until chicken is cooked through and lentils are tender, 20-25 minutes.

Remove chicken and let cool slightly before shredding. Return chicken to pot and add parsley if using. Remove from heat and stir in lemon juice. Serve with jammy onions and garlic and a dollop of yogurt. Add an extra slice of lemon to each bowl for some additional brightness. Add siracha or hot sauce for an extra kick!

I know it seems crazy to just use water and not chicken broth but the soup was still very tasty! You could absolutely use chicken broth if you prefer.
