I searched the internet for it ... to no avail.  If you recognize it or are able to find it online.. let me know so I can give credit where credit is due !  I followed this recipe almost exactly.  I added a couple cloves of minced garlic (because garlic makes everything better) and  I used a can of hot Rotel tomatoes instead of the canned tomatoes... but..we love spicy food.  Always go with your own preferences.  If you have a favorite canned tomato... try it out. I bet it will be great! The same goes for the garbanzo beans.  If this is not something you love, try kidney or black beans.  Any canned bean will do the trick.

Ingredients

1 medium onion, chopped 

2 cloves of garlic minced (optional)

1 cup of carrots, chopped

1 cup of celery, chopped

1 cup mushrooms, sliced

8 oz boneless chicken breasts, cut in bite sized pieces

1 cup brown lentils

1 14.5 oz can garbanzo beans, rinsed and drained (or other canned bean)

1 14.5 oz can chopped tomatoes (or Rotel)

3 cups chicken broth

2 cups of water

2 tsp cumin

1 tsp oregano

1 tsp kosher salt

1/4 tsp black pepper

Directions

Coat the bottom of a large pot with cooking spray or olive oil. Saute the chicken pieces until lightly browned. Remove chicken from pan.  Add onion and garlic to pot and saute until onion is soft. Add carrots, celery and mushroom. Saute a few minutes longer.  Add chicken, lentils, garbanzo beans, tomatoes, chicken broth, spices, salt and pepper.  Bring to a boil.  Cover, reduce heat and simmer for one hour. DONE! Serve with a crusty bread and salad and you have got a great meal!
