 This recipe has great flavor, comes together really quickly, and would go great with the sechuan noodles, over some white rice, or all by themself!



Ingredients:
2 tbsp soy sauce
2 tbsp water
1 tbsp dry sherry
1 tsp sugar
1/2 tsp cornstarch
1/4 tsp ground white pepper
1/4 tsp red pepper flakes
1/4 tsp dry ground mustard
2 tbsp vegetable oil
1 lb green beans, trimmed and cut into pieces
1/4 lb ground pork
3 garlic cloves, minced
1 tbsp grated fresh ginger
3 scallions, white and light green parts only, sliced thin
1 tsp toasted sesame oil

Directions:
1. Stir together soy sauce, water, sherry, sugar, cornstarch, white pepper, pepper flakes, and dry mustard in small bowl until sugar dissolves.

2. Heat oil in a nonstick skillet over high heat until just smoking.  Add beans and cook, stirring frequently, until crisp-tender and skins are shriveled and blackened in spots, 5-8 minutes.  Transfer beans to a large plate.

3. Reduce heat to medium high and add pork to skillet. Cook, breaking pork into small pieces, until no pink remains, about 2 minutes. Add garlic and ginger and cook 15-20 seconds.

4. Stir sauce to recombine and return beans to skillet with sauce. Toss and cook until sauce is thickened, 5-10 seconds.

5.
Remove from heat and stir in scallions and sesame oil. Serve immediately.
