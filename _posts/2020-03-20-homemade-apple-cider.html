Apple Cider
Ingredients
10-12 apples, cored (not peeled) and quartered
2 oranges, peeled and quartered
4 cinnamon sticks
16 cups of water
1 whole nutmeg
2 tsp whole cloves
2 tsp whole allspice
1 inch fresh ginger, scrubbed and sliced
1/2 cup maple syrup
1/4 cup brown sugar

 Directions
1. Add all ingredients except the maple syrup and brown sugar to a large stock pot. Make sure you have an inch or two of headspace at the top of your pot.

2. Heat over high heat and bring to a boil. Reduce to medium-low, cover, and simmer for around two hours, until apples are completely soft.

3. Mash apples and oranges to release additional juice/flavor, then cover and simmer for another hour.

4. Strain out solids starting with a larger mesh sieve and working down to a fine-mesh sieve or even cheese cloth.

A few notes: I usually do one pass with a large sieve to remove the solids, then one to two passes with a fine mesh sieve to remove any smaller apple solids. When using the fine mesh sieve, try not to press the liquid through the sieve; it will push through grittier solid bits that prevent your cider from having a smooth texture. Instead, swirl the liquid to encourage it to pass through the sieve, and rinse/empty your sieve regularly to get those small-grain apple solids out of the way.

5. Return to heat and add sweetener to taste (above are only guidelines, you could use honey or white sugar!)

6. Serve warm or refrigerate. It will last a few weeks in the fridge or can be frozen. Whenever you want to serve, thaw or rewarm on the stove.  You can garnish with orange slices and cinnamon stick, and pair with splash whiskey for a kick!
