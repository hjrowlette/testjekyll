 This is a super good winter salad recipe! I love a baked salad and this one is a nice spin on classic caesar that's a little healthier and tastier in my opinion. If you make the full amount of brussel sprouts or cabbage it'll probably take 4 sheet pans, so I'd plan on doing two rotations in the oven. You could easily cut it in half with a smaller cabbage or fewer sprouts, and it'd probably make 2 servings. enjoy!

Caesar-y Brussel Sprout Salad with Za’atar Chickpeas

-serves 4-


Ingredients

1 (15-ounce) can of chickpeas

1-2 heaping tsp za'atar seasoning

2 & 1/2 lbs brussel sprouts or cabbage

Olive oil as needed

Kosher salt & fresh cracked black pepper as needed

1 2oz tin anchovies, oil packed

2 cloves garlic minced or grated with a microplane

1/2 cup tahini

1 lemon

1/2 cup flat leaf parsley, roughly chopped

1/4 cup cold water

1/2 ounce freshly grated parmesan optional, for topping

 

Directions

1. Preheat your oven to 400°F.

2. Drain and rinse the chickpeas. Dry them well before adding them to a food processor. Pulse until they form chickpea "crumbs." Spread these out evenly on a baking sheet. Generously drizzle the chickpea crumbs with olive oil, toss in the za'atar and season with salt. Toss with your hands until everything is coated. Bake for 25-30 minutes until dry and crisp while you prep your brussel sprouts/cabbage.

3. Thinly slice the brussel sprouts (mandolin works great here) and add them to baking sheets. You want them to have enough space on the pan to breathe and crisp up, so feel free to use a couple of baking sheets. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.

4. Once the chickpeas come out of the oven, put the brussel sprouts in and bake for 20-22 minutes.

While the brussel sprouts are roasting, make the anchovy tahini sauce.

5. Put a small saucepan on medium low heat. Add in just the oil from the tin of anchovies, and let it heat up. Then add the anchovies to the pan, they should immediately sizzle and begin to "melt" into the oil.

Add the grated garlic into the pan with the anchovy oil. Cook on medium heat for 2-3 minutes or until you can no longer taste any raw garlic. This will look like a thick paste.

6. Add this paste, the tahini, the juice from one lemon, and the parsley to a blender or food processor. Blend to combine. It will get thick, so add water to dilute the mixture into a dressing.

7. Remove the greens from the oven. Drizzle a good amount of the dressing onto the greens and give them a toss.

8. To serve, pile the greens into bowls. Sprinkle the chickpea crumbles on top. Grate on some freshly grated parmesan. Serve immediately.
