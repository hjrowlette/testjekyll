 Another winner wnner chicken dinner from Best of America by Carla Calpalbo and Laura Washburn published in 1994.  This will spice up your life!  Less spicy? Use less cayenne, also try this on bone in chicken pieces. 


Ingredients

3.5     lb whole chicken
          juice of one lemon
4        garlic cloves, minced
1        Tbsp cayenne pepper
1        Tbsp paprika
1        Tbsp oregano
1/2    tsp course black pepper
2        tsp (plus some)
1        tsp salt


Directions

With a sharp knife or poultry shears, remove the backbone. Turn the chicken breast side up, with the heel of your hand, press down to break the breast bone, and open the chicken flat like a book.  Insert a skewer through the chicken, at the thighs, to keep it flat during cooking.

Place chicken in a shallow dish and pour over the lemon juice.

In a small bowl, combine the garlic, cayenne, paprika, oregano, pepper, and oil. Mix well.  Rub mixture evenly all over chicken, adding extra oil if necessary to make it spreadable.

Cover and let marinate 2-3 hours at room temperature or refrigerate overnight. (Return chicken to room temperature before roasting).

Season the chicken with salt on both sides. Transfer it to a shallow roasting pan.

Put the chicken in a cold oven and set the temperature to 400F.  Roast until the chicken is done, about an hour, turning occasionally and basting with pan juices.  To test for doneness, prick with a skewer: juices that run out should be clear.
