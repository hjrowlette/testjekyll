Mayak Eggs (Korean Drug Eggs)

Ingredients

6 large eggs - cold or room temperature

Optional- sesame oil

 

Marinade

½ cup soy sauce

1 Tablespoon dark soy sauce - (optional); substitute with soy sauce

½ cup water

¼ cup sugar, honey or agave

2 cloves garlic - minced

3 stalks scallions - chopped

3 thai chile peppers - red or green; sliced (could substitute jalapeno or serrano, just slice very thinly)

1 Tablespoon sesame seeds

 

Directions

1. Make marinade: Mix all the marinade ingredients in a container and set aside.

2. Soft boiled eggs: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Gently lower each egg into the pot. Set a timer for 6 minutes for room temperature eggs or 7 minutes for cold eggs. Ensure eggs are in a single layer while boiling.

3. -optional-Stir the eggs in one direction for the first 30 seconds for an even cross section.

4. After timer is up, place the eggs in an ice bath to stop cooking. Once cooled, peel eggs.

5. Add the eggs to the container with the marinade. For best flavor, let it marinate overnight in the fridge.

6. To serve, cut each egg in half. Optionally drizzle toasted sesame oil on top before enjoying.

Eggs last 3 to 4 days in the fridge in an airtight container.

How to use leftover marinade: Use the extra marinade in salad dressings, as a dipping sauce for dumplings, or you can reduce it and use as a glaze.
