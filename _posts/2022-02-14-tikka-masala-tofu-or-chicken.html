WHAT A RECIPE! I'll preface this by saying I have only cooked this with tofu, not chicken, but chicken tikka masala is also very much a classic so I want this post to cover both! This is so warming and delicious, a homerun winter dinner you can prep the day ahead to make it pretty easy to cook the night of. 


Ingredients

Tofu or Chicken

3 boneless skinless chicken breasts or 2 12 to 14-ounce blocks extra firm tofu

2/3 cup 2% plain Greek yogurt* (do not use nonfat)

2 tablespoons lemon juice

2 tablespoons minced fresh ginger

3-6 cloves minced garlic

2 teaspoons ground coriander

2 teaspoons garam masala

2 teaspoons ground cumin

2 teaspoons paprika

1 teaspoon kosher salt

2 tablespoons water

 

Sauce

3 tablespoons oil

1 large onion, finely chopped

2 tablespoons minced ginger

8 cloves garlic, minced

2 teaspoons ground cumin

2 teaspoons ground turmeric

2 teaspoons ground coriander

2 teaspoons paprika

2 teaspoons chili powder

2 teaspoons garam masala

1 tablespoon tomato puree

28oz can or 3 ½ cups tomato puree (I use a can of whole peeled tomatoes and crush them myself)

1 ¼ cups water

1 cup heavy cream


Serving

¼ cup fresh cilantro, for garnish

cooked rice, for serving

naan bread, for serving

 

Directions

1. If using tofu, drain and press tofu (don’t press so hard that the tofu breaks apart though).  Slice the tofu or chicken into bite sized chunks.

2. Combine all ingredients in a bowl and stir until chicken/tofu is well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour or overnight.

3. When ready to bake tofu, place the racks in the upper and lower thirds of your oven and preheat the oven to 400 degrees F. Line two rimmed baking sheets with parchment paper and lightly coat the paper with nonstick spray. Arrange the tofu pieces on the baking sheets in a single layer so that they are not touching one another (discard any extra marinade). Bake in the upper and lower thirds for 15 minutes, then remove the pans from the oven, flip the pieces over and return the pans to the oven, switching their positions on the upper and lower racks. Bake for 15 additional minutes, until tofu is deeply golden.

4. When ready to bake chicken, Preheat the oven to 500°F (260°C). Line a high-sided baking pan or roasting tray with parchment paper. Place the marinated chicken pieces on bamboo or wooden skewers, then set them over the prepared baking pan, making sure there is space underneath the chicken to help distribute the heat more evenly. Bake for about 15 minutes, until slightly dark brown on the edges. You could also just pan cook your chicken if you prefer

5. While your tofu or chicken is cooking start the sauce: Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat, then sauté the onions, ginger, and garlic until tender but not browned. Add the cumin, turmeric, coriander, paprika, chili powder, and garam masala and stir constantly for about 30 seconds, until the spices are fragrant. Stir in the tomato puree, tomato sauce, and 1 ¼ cups of water, then bring to a boil and cook for about 5 minutes. Pour in the cream.

6. Add chicken/tofu to the sauce, cooking for another 1-2 minutes. Garnish with cilantro and serve over rice or alongside naan bread.
