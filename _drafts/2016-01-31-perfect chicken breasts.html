How To Cook Moist & Tender Chicken Breasts Every Time


Ingredients

1 to 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, of similar size
Salt and freshly ground black pepper
1 tablespoon olive oil, unsalted butter, or combination of both

Instructions

  1. Pound the chicken breasts to an even thickness with the bottom of a wide jar or glass. You can also (carefully!) use the handle of a heavy chef's knife.
  2. Lightly salt and pepper the chicken breasts.
  3. Heat the pan over high heat. When it is quite hot, add the olive oil (or butter, if using). Swirl the pan so it is lightly covered with the olive oil.
  4. Turn the heat to medium-high. Add the chicken breasts. Cook for just about 2 minutes to help them get a little golden on one side.
  5. Flip each chicken breast over. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  6. Turn the heat to medium-low, and cover pan with a tight-fitting lid. Set a timer for 15 minutes, and walk away. Do not lift the lid.
  7. After 10 minutes have elapsed, turn off the heat. (If you have an electric stove, remove the pan from the heat.) Reset the timer for 10 minutes and leave the chicken breasts in the pan. Do not lift the lid
  8. After the 10 minutes are up, take the lid off, and your chicken is done. Make sure there is no pink in the middle of the chicken breasts.
